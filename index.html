Bio.

Paramore:
Rocking the World Since 2004.

Paramore is an American rock band formed in Franklin, Tennessee, in 2004. The band currently consists of lead vocalist Hayley Williams, guitarist Taylor York, and drummer Zac Farro. They gained mainstream success with their second album, Riot! (2007), which included hits like “Misery Business” and “Crushcrushcrush.”

Over the years, Paramore has released several successful albums, including Brand New Eyes (2009) and their self-titled album Paramore (2013), which featured the Grammy-winning single “Ain’t It Fun.” Despite lineup changes, the band has maintained a strong fan base and continues to be a significant influence in the rock and alternative music scenes.

In 2023, Paramore released their sixth studio album titled “This Is Why”. This album marked their return after a hiatus of several years since their previous work, After Laughter (2017). This Is Why includes singles like “This Is Why,” “The News,” “C’est Comme Ça,” and “Running Out of Time.” The album received critical acclaim and debuted at number two on the Billboard 200.

Additionally, in October 2023, they released a remix album titled “Re: This Is Why”, featuring remixed and reimagined versions of the original album’s songs by various artists.

Zac Farro is the drummer and one of the founding members of Paramore, born on June 4, 1990, in Voorhees Township, New Jersey. He and his brother, Josh Farro, formed the band alongside Williams and others in 2004. Zac initially left Paramore in 2010 but returned in 2017, contributing to the band's renewed energy and creativity.


His drumming is characterized by its intensity and precision, playing a key role in Paramore's distinctive sound. In addition to drumming, Zac is involved in songwriting and has worked on various side projects, showcasing his versatility as a musician.

Hayley Williams is the lead vocalist of Paramore and was born on December 27, 1988, in Meridian, Mississippi. She moved to Franklin, Tennessee, during her childhood, where she began pursuing music at a young age. In 2004, at just 15 years old, she formed Paramore with friends from school, and her powerful voice quickly became the band's signature.

Over the years, Williams has been recognized for her dynamic stage presence and emotional lyrics, which resonate with fans worldwide. Beyond her work with Paramore, she has also ventured into solo projects, releasing her debut solo album, "Petals for Armor," in 2020.

Taylor York is the lead guitarist of Paramore, born on December 17, 1989, in Nashville, Tennessee. He joined the band in 2007, bringing a unique sound that helped shape the group's musical evolution. Before Paramore, York was involved in various local bands and had a strong interest in music production.

His guitar work and songwriting contributions have been crucial to the band's success, particularly on albums like "Paramore" (2013) and "After Laughter" (2017). Outside of the band, York has collaborated with other artists and is known for his introspective lyrics and innovative musical style.

Album.

Paramore's latest album, "This Is Why," marks a bold evolution in their sound and lyrical depth. The band seamlessly blends their signature pop-punk roots with elements of indie and alternative rock, creating a fresh yet familiar vibe. Lyrically, the album explores themes of anxiety, self-discovery, and the complexities of modern life, reflecting the struggles and triumphs of their journey as artists.

With standout tracks that showcase Hayley Williams' powerful vocals and the band's tight instrumentation, "This Is Why" is both a celebration of their growth and a testament to their enduring relevance in the music scene. Fans will appreciate the raw honesty and infectious energy that Paramore continues to deliver.
